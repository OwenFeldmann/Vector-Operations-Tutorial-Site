Vector Normalization

A vector v consists of a length or magnitude and a direction. A normalized vector for v has a length of 1, but the same direction as v. This can be calculated by dividing a vector by its magnitude. 

	v / |v| = [v1 / |v| , v2 / |v| , ... , vn / |v|]

An easy way to calculate the magnitude of a vector is to take the square root of the dot product with itself. 

	|v| = root(v•b)
