Scalar Vector Multiplication

The scalar multiplication of a vector v by a scalar K can be found by multiplying all vector elements by the scalar factor. 

	v*K = v1*K + v2*K + ... + vn*K

Tutorial Use

Note: A random scalar between 1.0 and 2.0 is chosen for each vector drawn.

On the canvas on the left, define your vectors by clicking the starting point, and the the ending point for each vector. After both vectors are defined, clicking again will clear the vectors and define the starting point of the new first vector. The values of the vectors and the result of the scalar multiplication operation are displayed in the canvas once both vectors are defined. If you cannot see the second scaled vector, please draw your original vector in the bottom left quadrant.
