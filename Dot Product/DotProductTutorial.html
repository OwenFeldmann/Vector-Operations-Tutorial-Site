Dot Product

The dot product of two vectors v and u can be found by summing the product of the cooresponding vector elements. 

	v•u = v1*u1 + v2*u2 + ... + vn*un

An alternative method to find dot product is the multiply the magnitudes of both vectors and the cosine of the angle between the vectors. 

	v•u = |v||u|cos(θ).

This leads to the fact that v•u = 0 iff v and u are orthogonal. Note that the zero vector 0 is orthogonal to every vector. Since the vector components are readily available, this site uses the first method to calculate the dot product.
