Cross Product

The cross Product of two vectors v and u can be found by multiplying and subtracting a specific sequence of vector elements. To determine the direction of the new vector we can utilize the Right Hand Rule. 

	v×u = ((v2*u3) - (v3*u2), (v3*u1) - (v1*u3),(v1*u2) - (v2*u1))

An alternative method to find cross Product is the multiply the magnitudes of both vectors, the sine of the angle between the vectors, and n which is the unit vector that is at a right angle to both vectors. 

	v×u = |v||u|sin(θ)n.

This leads to the fact that v•u = 0 iff v and u are orthogonal. Note that the zero vector 0 is orthogonal to every vector. Since the vector components are readily available, this site uses the first method to calculate the cross Product.
